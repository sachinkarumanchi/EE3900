\section*{Quadratic forms Q2.34}
Find The area enclosed by the ellipse
\begin{align}
    \vec{x}^T\myvec{\frac{1}{a^2}&0\\0&\frac{1}{b^2}}\vec{x}&=1
\end{align}
\section*{Solution}
Given the equation of ellipse
\begin{align}
    \vec{x}^T\myvec{\frac{1}{a^2}&0\\0&\frac{1}{b^2}}\vec{x}&=1
\end{align}
On comparing with the standard form we get 
\begin{align}
    \vec{c}&=\myvec{0\\0}\\
    \vec{D}&=\myvec{\frac{1}{a^2}&0\\0&\frac{1}{b^2}}\\
    \vec{u}^T\vec{V}^{-1}\vec{u}-f &= 1\\
    \lambda_1&=\frac{1}{a^2}\\
    \lambda_2&=\frac{1}{b^2}
\end{align}
Semi major and minor axes of ellipse are
\begin{multline}
    a=\sqrt{\frac{\vec{u}^T\vec{V}^{-1}\vec{u}-f }{\lambda_1}}
    =\sqrt{\frac{1}{\frac{1}{a^2}}}
    =a
\end{multline}
\begin{multline}
    b=\sqrt{\frac{\vec{u}^T\vec{V}^{-1}\vec{u}-f }{\lambda_2}}
    =\sqrt{\frac{1}{\frac{1}{b^2}}}
    =b
\end{multline}
The Equation of ellipse can be written as 
\begin{align}
    \frac{x^2}{a^2}+\frac{y^2}{b^2}&=1
\end{align}
Therefore, the area enclosed by the  ellipse would be $\pi a b$
